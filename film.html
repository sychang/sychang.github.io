FILM AND VIDEO EDITING
Broadcast Journalism, Short Films, and Montages
Project Summary
Impact and opportunity: For three years in high school, I was a member of "Central Intelligence (CI)," our school's bi-weekly, student-produced television news program. The shows we created aired to both our school as well as the local community. For a semester, I was also in CalTV, Berkeley's online television station.

My role: For each year of CI, I was a reporter and videographer throughout the year. During my second, year, I was the school's lead Memories Video producer, and I was in charge of planning, filming, and editing an hour-long video for the student body to commemorate our school year. In my third year, I was the line editor for the news program. In addition to producing my own bi-weekly stories, I collected and quality checked the rest of the staff's stories. I inserted graphics and anchor segments between the stories to put together the whole news show.

Creating A Show: For CI, my partner and I would pitch a story topic at the start of every month, set up interviews around the school and community, film the interviews, then write a voiceover script based on those interview sound bites, film appropriate video clips, or b-roll, to lay over the audio, then edit the pieces together in Final Cut Pro. Below is an example of one of our news stories, investigating "Girls On The Run," an after-school program that teaches elementary schoolers the importance of physical and mental health.

Another type of story is the feature story/documentary that details our subjects on a more personal level. This one is about "Coping With Cancer," looks into the lives of two students at our school who lost their parents to cancer.

A final feature story details "The Greatest Generation," a group of WWII veterans who are making a difference in the classroom.

Montage Editing: At the end of every school year, CI creates an hour-long "Memories Video" to commemorate the year's events. As part of its production, I was in charge of editing a montage documenting our entire student body. My team and I filmed over 4 hours of video footage to capture the "Faces" of our student body. After filming, I cut the song mix and edited the clips together in a fast-paced and creative manner - these took over 50 hours of work each, but was probably my favorite part of CI. Challenges and Things I Learned

Working with best friends and strangers: Through this program, I learned to understand strangers quickly, whether I was working with them or interviewing them. On the flipside, some of my most challenging teamwork experiences came when I was working with close friends. This program taught me how to be considerate, but assertive of my ideas, in order to produce our best work the most efficiently.

Deadlines: Throughout the school year, we had bi-weekly deadlines, and I learned to appreciate deadlines and work efficiently under pressure. I learned how to creatively simplify tasks without sacrificing work level.

Attention to detail: I hate putting in half-effort for projects, and I gained a strong attention to detail by constantly editing. If a pause between interview sound bites was a split second too long or the camera was tilted during a shoot, it noticeably interrupted the rhythm of the story, so I paid great attention to detail when editing.

Asking questions that really matter: I believe broadcast journalism has a strong connection to design, because in both fields, there is storytelling involved - whether it's to convey a design or report about someone's experience. I learned that asking the right questions helped understand interviewees better return a much more meaningful narrative.
