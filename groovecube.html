GROOVECUBE (CHANGE THE SPACING IN CSS)
A Virtual Collaborative Dance Experience // Critical Practices Fall 2015
Project Summary
Impact and opportunity: Often the most intimitading part of hip hop dance for beginners and experienced dancers, alike, is freestyling, or dancing without preparation or choreography in mind. Nonetheless, freestyling is important in helping dancers build their skills and develop their style. We wanted to create an experience that allows members of the dance community to collaborate virtually and put together a music video without the intimidation of a large live freestyle circle.

Concept: GrooveCube is an acrylic cube enclosure that records multiple people dancing to different parts of a song. After a song is complete, the cube concatenates the videos together, overlays music, and uploads the finished music video to YouTube for the dancers to view. Features include:

  •  A button to trigger a light countdown, after which a music clip plays and the camera module records the user dancing from the cube's POV.
  •  UPLOAD TO YOUTUBE? MAYBE RESTRUCTURE THIS SECTION A BIT.
  •  A token store where children can exchange tokens for upgrades to their companion character.

My role: I was a member in a group of 9 undergraduates. Throughout the project, I interviewed users, illustrated multiple mockup iterations, and created the video for the project.

The Process
Questions we wanted to answer

  •  What is the biggest challenge facing children with diabetes??
  •  How can we incorporate technology that is simplistic enough that 5 year olds can use it and not be distracted by it?
  •  How can we create something that's unobtrusive to daily life?
Questions we asked our users (diabetic children and parents)
  •  What is the biggest challenge with having Diabetes?
  •  What do you usually do when you aren't feeling well at school?
  •  What is the biggest difference that Diabetes has made in your life?
How we developed our solution: Initially the project prompt was to create an educational children's toy. Our team wanted to take the definition of "education" outside the classroom and wanted to tackle general health education. We saw a need with Diabetes, because no product in the current market can accompany a young student into the classroom and encourage a healthier lifestyle.

From our research, we developed personas of Diabeted children as well as parents and teachers who monitor the children. We began brainstorming solutions for our personas, which ranged anywhere from insulin pens to Club Penguin-like communities. After combining and narrowing down the ideas, we decided to create a Tamagotchi-like experience on a smart watch interface that could accompany a student anywhere without being obstrusive. A major pain point for children is their need to rigorously document their food intake. We sought to gamify and simplify the experience for them and give parents peace of mind that their health is being monitored. We sketched low-fidelity interfaces and interactions for our watch, a sample shown below.

After fleshing out the content and functionality with these sketches, we were able to mock them up digitally to test how they would appear on a watch screen. Some of the Illustrator mockups are shown below.

With these product features, Diabetic children don't have to feel like they are fighting the disease alone, and can be incentivized and empower to maintain healthy lifestyle habits. For more information on the watch features, visit the project website I created.
