Product Design, Berkeley Innovation
Spring 2014
Currently, many impoverished rural Filipino families burn expensive and harmful kerosene to supply light from dusk to dawn. Our challenge? Design an affordable, sustainable, and user friendly communal light fixture using alternative energy sources.
My Role
With my team, I conducted user research and obtained information regarding rural Filipino lifestyle. I was responsible for many of our design ideas early on during brainstorming, suggested the extendable feature of our light, and built the mid-fidelity prototype of our eventual design. I created the graphics for our final showcase presentation.
The Product
Our resulting product concept, KIN, is a multi-functional, communal, and portable solar-powered light fixture. Each individual light begins as a collapsed, upright lamp that can be used for studying or local tasks such as weaving crafts. The light may be inverted, expanded, and hung from the ceiling as a communal room fixture. If a family has multiple lights, they may be stretched, strung together, and mounted on a wall to serve as a decorative but functional fixture. The solar panel lies on the outer side of the base, and the light can be easily charged by hanging it outside on a clothesline, for example. From our research, we concluded that a cell phone charger is very important for citizens in our target community, so we designed a built-in USB port at the base of the light that can charge a connected cell phone.
